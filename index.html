Impact of Cross-Validation on Machine Learning Models for Early Detection of Intrauterine Fetal Demise

This research addresses the global concern of intrauterine fetal demise by using machine learning models to detect fetal health, achieving 99% accuracy through cross-validation, particularly with Gradient Boosting and Voting Classifier. The study also explores Blackbox evaluation techniques to understand model mechanisms and feature selection.

Healthy-unhealthy animal detection using semi-supervised generative adversarial network

This study introduces a novel deep learning approach for the detection of healthy and unhealthy animals in images, achieving an impressive 91% accuracy through image augmentation and a semi-supervised generative adversarial network (SGAN). This innovative method significantly advances the field of animal health detection in computer vision applications.

Taxi Origin-Destination Demand Prediction using DNN Algorithm with NYU Database

This paper introduces the novel concept of predicting taxi demand between all-area pairs, going beyond traditional methods that focus solely on taxi origin locations. It demonstrates the superior performance of a Deep Neural Network (DNN) in capturing demand patterns compared to conventional AI techniques, through extensive testing on a large dataset.
